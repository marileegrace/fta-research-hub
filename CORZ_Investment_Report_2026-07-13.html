Executive Summary & Investment Thesis

▾
BULL. Core Scientific is no longer a bitcoin miner — it is a contracted AI-data-center landlord in disguise, and the market has not finished re-rating it. When shareholders rejected CoreWeave's $9B all-stock bid in October 2025, they didn't kill the story; they kept the $10.2B, 12-year take-or-pay backlog for themselves. At ~$23.45 the equity is priced closer to a cyclical crypto shell than to a 57%-gross-margin power-and-cooling REIT with 4.5 GW of pipeline. The gap between what CORZ is becoming and what it is quoted as is the alpha.

The core asymmetry: contracted colocation revenue is legally committed and largely insensitive to the bitcoin price that still dominates the ticker's reputation and its beta. As billable capacity ramps from 243 MW today toward the full 590 MW by early 2027, an annuity-like revenue stream north of $350M (colocation alone) switches on — against a self-mining segment the company is deliberately shrinking. The bear's strongest card is real and we do not dismiss it: extreme customer concentration in a single counterparty (CoreWeave) plus a freshly levered balance sheet ($3.3B of 7.75% notes). But those are known, priced, and structurally mitigated risks against an underappreciated, structurally growing cash engine.

Microeconomic & Valuation Analysis

▾

Industry & TAM

Core Scientific competes in the AI/HPC colocation and power-delivery layer — the "picks and shovels" beneath the neocloud boom. Independent estimates put global data-center capex on a multi-hundred-billion-dollar annual trajectory through the decade, with AI-ready, high-density (liquid-cooled) capacity the binding constraint. The relevant sub-market — contracted power delivery to GPU cloud operators — is expanding at a mid-to-high double-digit CAGR, gated not by demand but by available power, land, and construction speed. CORZ's differentiator is a portfolio of already-energized, grid-interconnected sites — the scarcest input in the entire stack.

Valuation Framing

On a trailing basis CORZ screens rich: ~$7.47B market cap on ~$355M TTM revenue implies a P/S ratio near 21x — "Excited" on the FTA scale (>18). But trailing revenue is the wrong denominator for a company mid-transition. On the full-ramp contracted run-rate (~$850M/yr implied by the $10.2B / 12-yr backlog), forward P/S compresses to ~8.8x — "Optimistic" (>8), and on an EV/contracted-MW basis (~$9.7B EV ÷ 590 MW ≈ $16M/MW) it sits below several pure-play peers. The valuation you see depends entirely on whether you underwrite the backlog — which is precisely the market inefficiency.

Qualitative Analysis — Moat, Management & Culture

▾

The Moat — Think in Watts, Not Servers

Core Scientific's moat is not software or brand; it is interconnected power at scale in the right places. The binding constraint on AI buildout is not GPUs — Nvidia will make more — but megawatts that are permitted, energized, and grid-connected today. CORZ controls a portfolio approaching 1.3 GW of operational/near-term capacity within a 4.5 GW gross pipeline, including large greenfield expansions at Muskogee (OK) and Pecos (TX) and a fresh ~430 MW land-and-power position in Hunt County, TX. Replicating this from a standing start takes years of utility queues and construction — a durable, time-based moat.

The second moat layer is the take-or-pay contract structure itself: a 12-year commitment where the customer pays whether or not it uses the capacity. That converts an operationally cyclical business into something closer to an infrastructure bond with equity upside — a systemic re-rating catalyst if the market ever fully recognizes it.

Management & Capital Allocation

Leadership under CEO Adam Sullivan steered the company through Chapter 11 restructuring (emerged early 2024) and pivoted decisively from bitcoin self-mining to HPC hosting — a genuinely contrarian, well-timed reallocation of infrastructure toward higher-margin demand. The willingness to walk away from a $9B acquisition (backed by the board and major holders) reflects conviction that standalone execution creates more value. Capital allocation is now aggressive-but-funded: the $3.3B notes raise pre-funds the buildout, trading balance-sheet risk for growth optionality.

Culture & Organizational Traits

Traits that matter here: failure tolerance (survived bankruptcy and a crypto winter and re-emerged), pace (contract-to-energization timelines measured in quarters), and customer intimacy — deep operational integration with a single anchor tenant. The obvious cultural risk is the flip side of that intimacy: an organization optimized around one customer. Diversifying the tenant base is the key organizational test of the next 12–24 months.

Founder-led? No — professional management post-restructuring rather than founder-run. This trades visionary intensity for operational discipline, which suits an infrastructure buildout but means the "mission lock-in" premium is lower.

Quantitative Analysis — Financial Deep Dive

▾

The financials tell a two-speed story: a collapsing legacy mining line and an exploding colocation line, with reported GAAP results distorted by large non-cash impairments as mining assets are written down. Reading through the noise is essential.

Revenue Streams, Explained Simply

1) High-density colocation (the future): CORZ rents powered, cooled data-center space to CoreWeave under long contracts. Q1'26 colocation revenue was $77.5M at a 57% gross margin — up from just $8.6M a year earlier (roughly +800% YoY). This is the annuity.

2) Digital-asset self-mining (the past): CORZ mines bitcoin on its own account. Q1'26 self-mining revenue was $30.1M at a negative gross margin — a segment being intentionally wound down and converted to higher-value hosting.

Profitability, Cash & Leverage

GAAP results are ugly on the surface — a Q1'26 net loss of $347.2M, driven by $266.5M of non-cash impairments on mining equipment. Strip those out and Adjusted EBITDA was modestly positive at +$4.4M, versus a negative print a year earlier — evidence the underlying business is crossing into normalized breakeven as colocation scales. Full-year 2025 revenue was $319.0M (down 38% as mining shrank) with the net loss narrowing 74% YoY.

Liquidity is ample post-raise: ~$1.0–1.15B cash against ~$3.3B of newly issued 7.75% senior secured notes due 2031, for roughly $2.2B net debt. Shares outstanding ~318M — dilution has been meaningful through the restructuring/growth phase and remains a watch-item, but the notes raise reduces near-term equity-issuance pressure.

Risks & Anti-Thesis — Steelman the Bear

▾

If I were short CORZ, here is the case I would make — engaged seriously, not as a strawman.

1. Single-customer concentration — the whole thesis rests on CoreWeave
Prob: HighImpact: SeverePriced in: Partially

Essentially 100% of colocation revenue and the entire $10.2B backlog depend on one counterparty that is itself a young, heavily-financed neocloud. If CoreWeave's own funding or demand falters, the "annuity" becomes a liability. Take-or-pay only protects you if the payer can pay.

2. Balance-sheet leverage at 7.75%
Prob: MediumImpact: HighPriced in: Partially

$3.3B of high-coupon secured notes fund a capital-intensive buildout. If energization slips or rates stay high, interest burden compounds against a business not yet consistently FCF-positive. Refinancing risk lands in 2031 but sentiment reacts sooner.

3. It still trades — and is beta'd — as a bitcoin miner
Prob: HighImpact: MediumPriced in: Yes

The re-rating thesis assumes the market reclassifies CORZ. It may not, for years. Crypto drawdowns will keep dragging the multiple and the residual mining segment bleeds negative-margin revenue in the meantime.

4. AI-infrastructure capex digestion / oversupply
Prob: MediumImpact: HighPriced in: No

The entire neocloud/colocation cohort is pricing in perpetual GPU scarcity. A capex pause by hyperscalers, or a glut of energized capacity in 2027–28, would compress lease economics on the 4.5 GW pipeline that today underwrites the bull case.

5. Execution & dilution risk on the growth pipeline
Prob: MediumImpact: MediumPriced in: Partially

Converting 4.5 GW of pipeline into contracted, energized MW requires flawless construction, utility interconnection, and likely additional capital. The share count has already grown materially; further equity issuance would cap per-share upside even if the business succeeds.

Net assessment: Risks 1 and 4 are the ones that can permanently impair capital; both are partially unpriced. Position sizing — not conviction alone — should reflect the fat left tail. This is a satellite, not a core, allocation.

Glossary & Methodology

▾
Colocation
Renting powered, cooled, physically-secure data-center space to a customer who installs their own compute (here, CoreWeave's GPUs). Why it matters: it converts CORZ's core asset — energized real estate — into recurring, high-margin rent. ↩ back to §2
Take-or-Pay Contract
A contract where the customer pays a committed amount whether or not it actually uses the capacity. Why it matters: it converts a cyclical hosting business into bond-like contracted cash flow — but only as strong as the payer's solvency. ↩ back to §3
Billable Capacity (MW)
Megawatts of data-center capacity that are energized, occupied, and actively generating revenue. Why it matters: the truest leading indicator of realized value for an infrastructure landlord — contracts only pay once MW are billing. ↩ back to §1
P/S Ratio (Price-to-Sales)
P/S = Market Cap ÷ Revenue Measures how many dollars investors pay per dollar of sales. FTA scale: >30 Euphoric · >18 Excited · >8 Optimistic · 4–8 Aware · <4 Cautious · <2 Pessimistic. Why it matters: for pre-profit growth infra, sales-based multiples are more meaningful than earnings — but the choice of trailing vs forward revenue changes the verdict entirely. ↩ back to §2
Adjusted EBITDA
EBITDA ± non-cash & one-off items Earnings before interest, tax, depreciation, amortization, adjusted for items like impairments and stock comp. Why it matters: for CORZ, GAAP is distorted by huge non-cash mining write-downs; Adj EBITDA reveals the normalized operating trajectory. ↩ back to §4
Annuity / Annuity-like Revenue
Predictable, recurring cash flow over a long, contracted horizon. Why it matters: the market pays a higher multiple for annuity streams than for cyclical ones — the crux of the re-rating thesis. ↩ back to §1
Enterprise Value (EV)
EV = Market Cap + Debt − Cash The takeover value of the whole business. Why it matters: EV/MW and EV/backlog normalize for CORZ's heavy debt, giving a truer cross-peer comparison than equity-only P/S. ↩ back to §2
